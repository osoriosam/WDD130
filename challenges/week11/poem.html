Poem: January

Author: John Updike

The days are short

The sun a spark,

Hung thin between

The dark and dark

Fat snowy footsteps

Track the floor.

Milk bottles burst

Outside the door.

The river is

A frozn place

Held still beneath

The trees of lace.

The sky is low

The wind is gray

The radiator

Purrs all day.
